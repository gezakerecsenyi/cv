Maginet

2023 -
Typescript, CSS

When I was chosen to be Editor-in-Chief for my school's Magazines Committee, it became apparent that mandating every designer on the team to purchase a copy of Affinity Publisher would not be a sustainable approach. Therefore, I embarked on the project of creating a free-to-use, open-source piece of publishing software, which seeks not only to implement but to improve upon many of my favourite features of industry-standard software like Affinity Publisher. Effectively just a giant Typescript project, implementing a program of this scale required creating custom viewport handlers and a component management system from scratch (as I opted not to use React or similar frameworks for this, given the difficulties they introduce with user-defined content). The centrepiece of the program is the custom property relationship definition tool, which takes the traditional concept of "master pages" to new heights, by allowing users to programmatically define - using a visual node-editor language - relationships that certain properties need to take and maintain between each other, such as the first letter of a paragraph being bigger, or the width of an image always being twice its height. This required many iterations to ensure all statements could be fully reversible (i.e. that if a user changed the height, the width could be calculated from this too) and the system could preempt and handle errors in the node 'code' in a user-friendly manner.

Hourbase

2022
Go, Firebase, MySQL, Node.js, Protobuf, GCP/AWS, Stripe, Kubernetes

Frustrated by the available offering of freelancing websites, we decided to design and develop a new type of freelance platform, whereby the site itself decides on socially-optimal prices for any given pairing of buyer and seller using an ELO-based algorithm. My role was to design the formulae, which numbered in the hundreds, for various actions on the site, including the effects of user interactions and local economic conditions (supply/demand for a given service on the site) on price, the effects of subject-specific and global ratings on buyer and seller ELO scores, and the effects of ELO on a given price match-up. I implemented these formulae in Go, while Node.js was selected for running the actual webserver for the site, with Protobuf streaming between the two servers. As the platform required a wide range of different processing features, ranging from payment handling through Stripe Connect, to image and video preview and processing, and even video calling capability, we adopted a microservice-based approach with Kubernetes clusters to manage the connection between them.
